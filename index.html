The Pit Bull Dilemma

How the nation's most popular dog became a shelter mainstay

By Sara Silvennoinen

Meet 8-year-old Bubba–also known as Bubbles. He's a Pit Bull Terrier and American Bulldog mix who has been up for adoption since 2019.

Bubba is one of nearly 190 pit bull terriers currently housed across 140 shelters in New York.

When looking at the primary breed of dogs in New York shelters, the pit bull terrier is the most common.

Around 30 percent of dogs in NYC shelters are so-called "bully breeds," a category that includes dogs such as American Pit Bulls, Cane Corsos, and Bullmastiffs.

According to experts, pit bulls are overrepresented in shelters primarily because of overbreeding and their negative reputation, both of which make it more difficult for them to be adopted.
